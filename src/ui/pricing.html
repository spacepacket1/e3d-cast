Cast Pricing (powered by E3D)

How credits work, what each tier unlocks, and what a render actually costs.

Credits

Cast is paid with E3D credits. 1,000 credits = 1 E3D or wE3D, before discounts. Buy credits with either Ethereum E3D or Base wE3D — both settle at the same rate. Holders of E3D get a 20% discount on credit purchases, applied automatically when you connect a qualifying wallet.

Credits are spent once, at job submission time — quoting a job is always free and never spends credits.

Tiers

Tiers set your size, length, duration, and retention limits. The Free tier is a safe preview: it never spends real credits on rendering and always runs as a watermarked dry-run, no matter what you submit — real renders start at Starter.

Why pasted transcripts have a length surcharge

When you paste transcript text instead of uploading audio, Cast synthesizes narration for the entire transcript before rendering — so the real cost scales with how much text you paste, not which preset you pick. The surcharge is 5 credits per 1,000 characters (4 per 1,000 for Pro), added on top of the base price above. A short paste of a few hundred characters barely moves the price; a Studio-tier transcript at the 200,000-character limit costs meaningfully more than a short one, in proportion to what it actually takes to produce.

Uploaded-audio jobs (Short, YouTube) don't carry this surcharge — their cost is already covered by the flat preset price above regardless of audio length, up to your tier's duration limit.

Example

A Starter-tier Transcript short job from a 2,000-character paste: 100 base + 10 surcharge (2 × 5) = 110 credits = 0.11 E3D.

Get exact numbers for your own job before you spend anything: quote it first with the API (POST /api/cast/jobs/quote — free, no credits spent) or from the Cast workspace.
